Suggested Economic Model

  • Consider the four economic agents as well as the Social Planner:
    • Power plant
    • Consumer
    • Government
    • Social Planner

Power plant

  • Power plant uses either clean energy or dirty energy to produce electricity (\(e\)).

  • Power plant has a continuum of plants, and she can freely adjust its relative proportion of dirty and clean electricity, \(\gamma\) and \(1-\gamma\), respectively.

  • Power plant’s total cost of producing electricity from dirty/clean energies consist of variable costs ( \(C_{d}(e)\) and \(C_{c}(e)\) ) and fixed costs ( \(F_{c}\) and \(F_{d}\) ):

\[ \begin{align} TC_{d} &= C_{d}(\gamma\times e) + F_{d}\\ TC_{c} &= C_{c}((1-\gamma)\times e) + F_{c} \end{align} \]

  • Taking the electricity price \(s\) as given, the power plant maximizes its profit by choosing how much to produce electricity \(e\) and the proportion of dirty energy power plant ( \(\gamma \in [0,1]\) ): \[ \begin{align} \max_{e, \gamma}\;\Pi_{e} &= s e - TC_{d}(\gamma \times e) - TC_{c}((1 - \gamma)\times e) \\ &= s e - C_{d}(\gamma \times e) - C_{c}((1 - \gamma)\times e) - F_{c} - F_{d} \end{align} \]

Consumer

  • Taking the electricity price \(s\) as given, consumer maximizes its utility by choosing a numeraire good \(x\) and an electricity consumption \(e\):

\[ \begin{align} \max_{x, e}\;U &= x + v(e)\\ \text{subject to }\; Y &= x + se \end{align} \]

The above problem is equivalent to: \[ \begin{align} \max_{e}\;U &= Y - se + v(e)\\ \end{align} \]

Electricity Market

In the electricity market, the electricity price \(s\) is such that the supply and the demand for electricity are met:

\[ v'(e) = \gamma C_{d}'(\gamma \times e) + (1 - \gamma) C_{c}'((1-\gamma)\times e) \]

Social planner

  • Social planner maximizes social welfare, which takes into account the damage caused by greenhouse gas emissions from dirty power plant, as a function of the amount of electricity produced by dirty power plant

\[ \begin{align} \max_{e,\gamma}\;SW &= \Pi_{e} + U - D\\ &= Y - F_{c} - F_{d} \\ &\quad+ v(e) - \, C_{d}(\gamma\times e) - \, C_{c}((1 - \gamma)\times e) - D(\gamma\times e), \end{align} \] where the damage \(D(\gamma\times e)\) increases with \(\gamma \times e\) at an increasing rate.

Government

  • Government can consider various policies:
    • Tax dirty power plants
    • Subsidy clean power plants
    • Setting emission standards to dirty power plants

Modeling ideas

  • For simplicity, here I abstract away from firm’s productions and emissions. Instead, I focus on only power plant and consumer.
  • If needed, you guys can always modify the model.
